Onpattro: Mechanism, Benefits, and What It Means for hATTR

Premier Hematology & Oncology
2025 · 5 min read

In recent years, Onpattro has emerged as a groundbreaking treatment option for individuals suffering from hereditary transthyretin amyloidosis (hATTR).

This rare and often debilitating condition can cause progressive nerve and heart damage, leading to a diminished quality of life.

Onpattro, with its innovative mechanism of action, has offered new hope for those diagnosed with hATTR, as it significantly alters the course of the disease and alleviates its symptoms.

But what exactly is Onpattro, how does it work, and what benefits does it offer to patients? Let’s take a deeper look into this transformative medication.

What is hATTR?

Hereditary transthyretin amyloidosis (hATTR) is a rare genetic disorder that is caused by mutations in the transthyretin (TTR) gene.

These mutations result in the production of abnormal transthyretin proteins, which are prone to misfolding and aggregation.

These misfolded proteins then accumulate in tissues and organs, primarily affecting the heart and peripheral nerves.

The buildup of amyloid deposits in these organs leads to a variety of symptoms, including neuropathy, cardiomyopathy, and in severe cases, organ failure.

The condition is progressive and can significantly reduce life expectancy if left untreated.

What is Onpattro?

Onpattro (patisiran) is an innovative medication that was specifically developed to target and treat hATTR. It is the first FDA-approved small interfering RNA (siRNA) therapy for this rare disease.

By targeting the underlying genetic cause of hATTR, Onpattro provides a much-needed solution for managing the disease and alleviating its symptoms.

Mechanism of Action

Onpattro works by using a mechanism known as RNA interference (RNAi) to silence the mutant TTR gene responsible for producing abnormal proteins.

RNA interference is a natural cellular process where small RNA molecules are used to regulate gene expression.

In the case of Onpattro, the medication introduces small interfering RNA (siRNA) molecules into the body, which specifically target and degrade the messenger RNA (mRNA) that encodes the mutant TTR protein.

By preventing the production of the abnormal transthyretin protein, Onpattro helps reduce the accumulation of amyloid deposits in the organs, ultimately slowing or halting the progression of the disease.

This is a revolutionary approach, as it directly addresses the root cause of the disease, rather than just managing its symptoms.

Benefits of Onpattro for hATTR

The introduction of Onpattro has been a game-changer for patients with hATTR. Here are some of the key benefits that make Onpattro a crucial treatment option:

  • Reduces Amyloid Deposition
    Onpattro works to reduce the buildup of amyloid deposits in vital organs such as the heart and nerves. This reduction in amyloid load helps prevent further damage to these organs, potentially preserving function and improving quality of life for patients.
  • Improved Neuropathy Symptoms
    One of the most debilitating aspects of hATTR is peripheral neuropathy, which leads to numbness, pain, and weakness in the limbs. Clinical trials have shown that Onpattro significantly improves neuropathy symptoms, with many patients experiencing enhanced motor function and a reduction in pain and numbness.
  • Cardiac Benefits
    hATTR often leads to cardiac amyloidosis, which results in heart failure and arrhythmias. Onpattro has been shown to improve cardiac function by reducing amyloid deposits in the heart, leading to a decrease in symptoms such as shortness of breath, fatigue, and irregular heartbeats.
  • Slows Disease Progression
    Onpattro is one of the few therapies that can slow the progression of hATTR, offering patients the opportunity to manage their condition more effectively. This can significantly improve long-term outcomes, especially when started early in the disease course.
  • Enhances Quality of Life
    As Onpattro reduces symptoms and prevents further organ damage, patients often experience a marked improvement in their quality of life. Many individuals report feeling more energetic, having less pain, and regaining some level of independence that was previously hindered by their symptoms.

What Does Onpattro Mean for hATTR Patients?

For patients living with hATTR, Onpattro represents a turning point in the management of the disease.

Historically, treatment options were limited, and the focus was primarily on managing symptoms rather than addressing the underlying cause.

With the advent of Onpattro, patients now have access to a therapy that can slow disease progression, improve symptoms, and even offer hope for those who previously had little to no treatment options.

Moreover, the availability of Onpattro highlights the importance of personalized, targeted therapies in modern medicine.

The treatment’s ability to directly address the genetic mutation at the core of hATTR makes it an exciting and transformative option for patients.

Conclusion

Onpattro is a revolutionary treatment that offers significant benefits for those living with hereditary transthyretin amyloidosis (hATTR).

By utilizing RNA interference technology to target the root cause of the disease, Onpattro helps prevent amyloid deposits from accumulating in vital organs, which in turn alleviates symptoms and improves quality of life.

As research continues to progress, Onpattro remains a beacon of hope for patients suffering from this rare and debilitating condition.
