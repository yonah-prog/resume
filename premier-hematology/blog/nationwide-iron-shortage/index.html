Nationwide Iron Shortage

Premier Hematology & Oncology
2024 · 5 min read

The United States is currently facing a significant shortage of IV iron, a crucial medication used to treat iron deficiency anemia in various patient populations. This shortage has widespread implications, particularly for patients with chronic kidney disease, cancer, gastrointestinal disorders, and other conditions that cause or exacerbate anemia.

Causes of the Shortage

  1. Increased Demand: There has been a surge in the demand for IV iron due to a rising number of patients diagnosed with conditions requiring iron supplementation. This demand outpaces the current production capacity of manufacturers.
  2. Manufacturing Issues: Several manufacturers have reported production delays and disruptions. These issues can be due to equipment failures, quality control problems, or delays in obtaining raw materials.
  3. Regulatory Challenges: The production of pharmaceuticals, including IV iron, is highly regulated. Compliance with stringent regulatory requirements can sometimes lead to delays in manufacturing and distribution.
  4. Supply Chain Disruptions: The COVID-19 pandemic has caused global supply chain disruptions, affecting the availability of raw materials and finished products. Shipping delays and logistical challenges continue to impact the pharmaceutical supply chain.

Impact on Patients and Healthcare Providers

  1. Treatment Delays: Many patients are experiencing delays in receiving necessary treatments, leading to worsening of their medical conditions.
  2. Increased Burden on Healthcare Providers: Clinics and hospitals are struggling to manage the demand for IV iron, often having to prioritize patients based on the severity of their condition.
  3. Switch to Alternative Treatments: Some healthcare providers are forced to switch patients to oral iron supplements or other alternative treatments, which may not be as effective or suitable for all patients.
  4. Referral to Other Providers: In some cases, patients are being referred to other healthcare providers or infusion centers that may have IV iron in stock, leading to increased travel and inconvenience for patients.

Actions Taken to Mitigate the Shortage

  1. Priority Distribution: Manufacturers are working with healthcare providers to prioritize the distribution of IV iron to the most critical patients.
  2. Increased Production: Efforts are being made to increase the production of IV iron, including expanding manufacturing facilities and speeding up production processes.
  3. Regulatory Flexibility: Regulatory agencies are providing some flexibility to expedite the approval and distribution of IV iron to address the shortage.
  4. Alternative Therapies: Research and development into alternative iron therapies and formulations are being accelerated to provide additional treatment options.

Premier Hematology and Oncology’s Response

At Premier Hematology and Oncology, we have a strong relationship with IV iron manufacturers, which allows us to receive priority shipments and maintain a steady supply. As a result, we are able to offer immediate availability of IV iron infusions, ensuring that patients do not have to wait for their treatments. Our practice has opened additional slots to accommodate new patients from other practices affected by the shortage, providing next-day appointments and a seamless onboarding process.
