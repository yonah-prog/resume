Two operating models

Sort it in-house.
Or hand it to us, end-to-end.

The recovery outcome is identical — measured diversion, certified destinations, audited reporting. The difference is whether the sorting happens on your campus or at our facility. We'll recommend one or the other after the audit, based on your space, your throughput and your team.

Option A On-site sorting facility

You sort. We design, operate & report.

The closed-loop model. Sorting happens at your campus, in infrastructure we design and run. Cleanest material, highest recovery value, and the strongest compliance narrative — because your waste never leaves your control until it's already sorted.

  • Site-level source-separation design & bin programme
  • On-site sorting station and (where viable) on-site composter producing agricultural-grade output
  • Multi-language staff training, signage and SOPs
  • Optional supply of compostable replacements for single-use items
  • Independent monthly audit and diversion certification
Best for · large campuses · industrial sites · multi-tenant parks Setup · 4–8 weeks
Option B Off-site processing

We collect. We sort. We report.

The managed model. You keep using a single pickup rhythm. We take everything to our facility, sort it stream by stream, route each fraction to its certified destination, and return one consolidated diversion report across all your sites.

  • Single, simple pickup contract — no operational change on your side
  • Sorting and recovery at our facility, with documented destinations
  • Consolidated multi-site reporting in one ESG-ready pack
  • We line up certified processors, recyclers and compost off-take
  • Same independent audit standard, off-site rather than on-site
Best for · hotels · multi-site groups · constrained back-of-house Setup · 2–4 weeks

Regulatory context · Israel

Why corporate diversion is now
the operational question.

Israel is moving from one of the most landfill-dependent waste systems in the OECD to one structured around sorting, recovery and treatment. Corporate-scale diversion programmes are the mechanism behind that policy — and the obligation is shifting from the integrated hauler to the operator.

80%
Israel's current landfill dependency — versus a 40% OECD average
20%
Mandated landfill share by 2030 under the National Waste Economy Strategy
14
Active alternative sorting & treatment facilities nationally — a documented capacity gap
6%
Share of national GHG emissions attributable to food waste alone
2021–2030 National Waste Economy StrategyThe headline framework mandating Israel's move from landfill to sorting, recovery and recycling — and the regulatory backdrop your programme will be assessed against.
Active Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)Compliance-ready handling required for packaging, batteries and e-waste. Corporate clients increasingly expected to evidence how their EPR streams are managed in practice.
2026 Inter-ministerial regulatory reviewA broader waste-sector regulatory process launched in early 2026, with the packaging market being re-opened to supervised competition.
Ongoing State Comptroller follow-up audit, 2025Found that existing landfill capacity is insufficient for projected volumes — accelerating the pressure on corporate-scale diversion programmes.
