The story

From practice squad to the front page.

DeMario Douglas arrived in New England with a nickname, a personality, and a following that was about to get a lot bigger. "Pop" Douglas spent his first NFL seasons not just earning targets — he was building a brand that would outlast any single contract. Brunswick was with him from the start.

The centerpiece was a custom soda collaboration with Avery's Beverages: the Pop Douglas Strawberry Cream Soda, launched with a proceeds commitment to Connecticut public schools. It's the kind of partnership that sounds simple and takes months to do right — sourcing the right brand partner, negotiating the structure, building the charitable component, and then getting it in front of the right press. Sports Illustrated covered it. Brunswick was named in the piece.

Off the field, Pop was everywhere. He was Guest of Honor at a Brazil vs. France World Cup Friendly at Gillette Stadium. He appeared on the Gronk & Edelman Podcast. He threw a Red Sox First Pitch. He starred in a Concepts x Nike x Patriots collab. And alongside teammate Anfernee Jennings, he donated 1,500 turkeys to Boys & Girls Clubs across Massachusetts — an initiative Brunswick organized that earned the team a second Sports Illustrated placement. In one season, his following went from 40,000 to 225,000. That's the work.
