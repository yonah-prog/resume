The story

The NIL flagship. The business architecture to match.

Boogie Fland transferred to the University of Florida in one of the most lucrative NIL deals in college basketball history — worth several million dollars. Brunswick negotiated it. Julian Aiken is named as his manager in ESPN's coverage of the commitment. This is the work Brunswick was built to do.

The deal wasn't just money — it was infrastructure. Brunswick managed the Gatorade campaign from shoot to distribution, producing branded graphics and editorial bench shots. It secured a fashion campaign with Mackage, placing Fland at the intersection of sport and style. And it put Boogie at the center of the NIL story nationally: ABC News featured him in an Impact x Nightline segment, now streaming on Hulu, about how NIL deals are reshaping what it means to be a student-athlete.

Fland was already considered one of the top guards in the country before Florida. What Brunswick brought was the business architecture around that talent — the deals, the press, the brand, the positioning. The NIL era created the opportunity. Brunswick turned it into a career.
